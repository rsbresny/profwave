Project Overview

With the world's population - and its energy needs - continuing to grow, the need to develop and use alternative, sustainable forms of energy is becoming increasingly important. Ocean waves offer an enormous and continuous source of energy that is renewable, environmentally friendly, and readily available near the majority of the world's most populated areas. The energy in ocean waves is converted into use able electrical energy by a device called a wave energy converter (WEC). This project will explore the potential of ocean wave energy as a new source of renewable energy. The project's overall goal is to develop a medium-sized WEC (5-10 foot length) during the 2016-17 academic year. The objectives during the Fall 2016 semester were to conduct research on WECs and to design our WEC and its power generation system. After successfully completing the design, securing funding and obtaining the proper permission to test the system, the Spring 2017 semester is being spent building the WEC and ultimately transporting it to the testing site for evaluation.
